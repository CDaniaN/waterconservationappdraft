Tips

1. USE LESS WATER WHEN BRUSHING YOUR TEETH/SHOWERING

An easy way to start saving water is to be mindful of turning your water off when brushing teeth, instead of keeping it running. Similarly, turning the shower off when washing your hair or shaving.

2. TAKE SHORTER SHOWERS

Showers can take up to 10 galons of water per minute. Reducing shower time can make a big difference in an effort to reduce your water usage.

3. CHECK PIPES FOR LEAKS

Water leaks can waste up to 50 gallons or more of unused water.

4. DON'T OVER WATER YOUR GARDEN

Most people dont know exactly how much water their garden needs, so they tend to over water. Educate yourself on how much water your garden actually needs.
